Enhance Your AI Voice Output with SSML (Speech Synthesis Markup Language)

Master the art of SSML customization to create more natural, expressive, and human-like AI-generated voices using Microsoft TTS. Whether you're building audiobooks, narrating YouTube videos, or creating accessible voice interfaces, SSML gives you the control to fine-tune your speech synthesis output.

Insert a Pause in Speech

Mary had a little lamb <break time="1s"/> whose fleece was white as snow.

Emphasize Specific Words

I told you I <emphasis level="strong">really like</emphasis> that place.

Adjust the Speaking Rate

Slow down for clarity: <prosody rate="slow">This is spoken slowly.</prosody>
Or speed up: <prosody rate="fast">Now we talk faster.</prosody>

Modify the Pitch

High pitch: <prosody pitch="+20%">This sounds higher.</prosody>
Low pitch: <prosody pitch="-20%">This sounds lower.</prosody>

Change the Volume

<prosody volume="x-soft">This is very soft.</prosody>
<prosody volume="loud">This is louder than normal.</prosody>

Use Microsoft TTS Voice Styles

<mstts:express-as style="cheerful">I'm feeling great today!</mstts:express-as>
Apply voice styles like "cheerful," "angry," or "sad" to better reflect emotional tones in speech.

Role-play Multiple Speakers

[speaker:John] How are you doing?
[speaker:Emily] I'm fine. Thank you!
Use labeled dialogue with SSML for immersive storytelling and conversational AI.
