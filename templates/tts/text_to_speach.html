Enhance Your Speech Output Using SSML and Multi-Voice Formatting:

Multi-Voice Dialogue Format

To assign different voices to each line of dialogue, use this format: 

[en-US-GuyNeural]: Hello there!
[en-GB-SoniaNeural]: Good morning!
[en-NG-EzinneNeural]: Welcome to the platform.

You can use any supported Microsoft TTS voice. See the full list of voices.

Insert a Pause

[en-US-GuyNeural]: Mary had a little lamb <break time="1s"/> whose fleece was white as snow.

Emphasize Words

[en-GB-SoniaNeural]: I told you I <emphasis level="strong">really like</emphasis> that place.

Adjust Speaking Rate

[en-US-GuyNeural]: <prosody rate="slow">This is spoken slowly.</prosody>
[en-US-GuyNeural]: <prosody rate="fast">Now we talk faster.</prosody>

Change Pitch

[en-NG-EzinneNeural]: <prosody pitch="+20%">This sounds higher.</prosody>
[en-NG-EzinneNeural]: <prosody pitch="-20%">This sounds lower.</prosody>

Change Volume

[en-US-GuyNeural]: <prosody volume="x-soft">This is very soft.</prosody>
[en-US-GuyNeural]: <prosody volume="loud">This is louder than normal.</prosody>

Use Voice Styles (If Supported)

[en-GB-SoniaNeural]: <mstts:express-as style="cheerful">I'm feeling great today!</mstts:express-as>

Need More Customization?

Explore full SSML support at Microsoft SSML Documentation.

Tips:

  • Use one voice per line, enclosed in square brackets like [en-US-GuyNeural].
  • Use SSML tags inline in each segment for fine control over speech output.
  • Make sure your input matches the required format: [VoiceName]: your message here.
